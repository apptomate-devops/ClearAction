Social sharing has decreased by 50 percent over the last two years
Darn!! We were just starting to get the hang of it! The Content Trends Report for 2018 from Buzzsumo has just been released. You can download a copy here. These are the highlights - taken directly from their report:
  • Social referral traffic declined in 2017 and was again overtaken by traffic from Google sites.
  • Average sharing of content on social networks fell due to increased competition, Facebook changes, and a rise in private sharing.
  • Based on a sample of 100 million posts published in 2017, 50% of all content gained just four shares or less across all the main social networks. This has halved since 2015 when the research was last done.
  • The BIG WINNERS? Sites that have built their reputation and authority. There has been a concentration of social sharing and some authoritative, quality sites that are actually seeing increases in social sharing.

Storynomics
Storynomics - Story-Driven Marketing in the Post-Advertising World, is the new book from best selling author of Story, Robert McKee and Skyword CEO, Thomas Gerace. The idea for the book came to Mr. McKee as he realized more and more "business people" were attending his screenwriting/acting seminars. They were there to learn how to tell a story. The book is the result of a two-year journey that had the duo delivering Storynomics lectures all over the globe. They would conduct the lecture, then wait for the audience's questions. They'd revise the content to include those questions/answers and give the lecture again. This went on until they stopped hearing pertinent questions and knew it was time to write the book.

The book is important to all marketing and CX professionals because it provides answers to today's biggest challenges on how to attract and retain ideal customers. The book outlines how marketing is changing. How up until now, marketing has pretty much been the same as it was in Benjamin Franklin's time. The role of marketing has been an iterative one, an optimizers role. But today, marketers must be taking risks and trying new channels making sure they're defining the new industry, as opposed to just playing in a space that was determined by their great, great grandparents.

They call for marketers to become Producers. Not in the sense of producing sales or even customers. Producers as in movie producers. Producers who run the show. They are not necessarily the storytellers. Their value will be assembling the storytelling team and giving them the resources they need to do their job effectively. Marketers will be the ones who set their story craft to help the team understand the core values. They will give storytelling assignments which are clear and articulate and designed in story form. They will assess the quality of the team's work and help them improve each individual piece so that these people can become better storytellers that are more aligned with the overall brand, over time.

Marketers will need to build a new Marketing Operational Plan because what used to work in an ad-world does not work today.
